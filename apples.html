APPLES

21 Aug 2018 - Jill Smith

An apple is a sweet, edible fruit produced by an apple tree. Apples are one of the most popular fruits worldwide and are consumed in various forms, including raw, cooked, or juiced.

Apple trees are cultivated worldwide, and are the most widely grown species in the genus Malus. The tree originated in Central Asia, where its wild ancestor, Malus sieversii, is still found today. Apples have been grown for thousands of years in Asia and Europe, and were brought to North America by European colonists.

There are thousands of apple cultivars, resulting in a wide range of colors, sizes, and flavors. Some popular varieties include Red Delicious, Granny Smith, Fuji, and Honeycrisp. Apples are a good source of fiber, vitamin C, and antioxidants.

Apples are often associated with health benefits, including heart health, digestive health, and blood sugar regulation. The saying "An apple a day keeps the doctor away" reflects the fruit's reputation for promoting good health.
